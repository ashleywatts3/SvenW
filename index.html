About Me

For my 8th birthday, I received a skateboard which would kick-off my dream of becoming a pro skater. For my 9th birthday, I received a camera so that my friends and I could make skate videos in hope of becoming sponsored by a skate brand. I was always the one behind the camera of course because all of my friends wanted to be in the video rather than recording it. We would ride through Concord looking for spots to get some cool footage. What could I really do other than make sure we had the sickest videos. Skate videos later turned into youtube videos that I would shoot and edit for us. Going into high school, a best friend of mine began making music which I would end up doing music videos for. He had suggested that I make a career out of it and I’m glad he did because look where I’m at now.

Growing up in Concord, an East Bay suburb, everyone in the area would attend Diablo Valley Community College. Many would end up majoring in business, or nursing, or something their parents told them to do. Not many people were into “art” or even saw it as a possibility as a career. Whenever I run into people from high school they ask me, “Hey you still doin that photography thing?”. I always laugh to myself then tell them that I am a filmmaker and yes I’m still doin it. It’s what I love to do and what I always want to do.
